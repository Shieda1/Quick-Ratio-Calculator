What is the quick ratio?
quick ratio is a finincial ratio that measures the short-term liquidity of a company by comparing the value of it's cash and current assets to it's short term liabilites. And sometimes it's called acid test.
How to calculate quick ratio?
To calculate quick ratio you should use the following formula:
quick ratio = current assets / current liabilities
where:
currest assets = cash equivalents + accounts receivable
example: company x's current assets are:
cash = $400,000
acounts receivable = $420,000
inventory = $765,000
marketable securities = $310,000
and it's current liabilities are:
short term debt = $212,000
accounts payables = $530,000
calculate the quick ratio of this company?
using the quick ratio formula the answer will be:
current assets = $400,000 + $420,000 + $765,000 + $310,000
current assets = $1,895,000
current liabilities = $212,000 + $530,000
current liabilities = $742,000
quick ratio = current assets / current liabilities
quick ratio = $1,895,000 / $742,000
quick ratio = 2.55
Why use quick ratio?
quick ratio is important because companies use it to know how well they will be able to pay their short term debts using only the most liquid of assets. It's also used to signals to internal management and investors if the company will run out of cash.
